# Exercise x All Outcomes — Consensus Reference: Synthesis

## Research Summary

A 3-cycle iterative research using the Eric Jang methodology with Structured Adversarial Ensemble produced a universal consensus reference on exercise's impact across 32 health outcomes.

**Cycle 1:** 5 parallel SCOUT streams covering mortality/CVD, cancer/metabolic, brain/mental health, musculoskeletal, and biomarkers/emerging outcomes. An adversarial CRITIC and a STATISTICIAN reviewed all 5 streams, identifying 6 red flags, unit inconsistencies, and positive-outcome bias.

**Cycle 2:** 3 targeted Deep Dives addressing: (DD-1) unified dose-response framework in MET-h/wk, (DD-2) two-tier evidence grading with null/negative findings, (DD-3) real-world implementation with injury risk, adherence data, and medication interactions.

**Cycle 3:** Synthesis into a master `consensus_reference.md` (843 lines, 32 outcomes) and independent FACT-CHECKER verification.

**Evidence base:** 28 key studies formally graded. 14 Grade A (50%), 9 Grade B/B+ (32%), 4 Grade C/C+ (14%), 1 Grade D (4%). 6 definitive Cochrane reviews. 4 landmark RCTs. Total pooled participants across key meta-analyses exceeds 5 million.

**Two-tier evidence grading** distinguishes RCT-based evidence (causal) from observational evidence (associational). All observational effect sizes carry an estimated 10-20% inflation from healthy user bias. RCT-derived estimates for subjective outcomes carry 5-15% inflation from blinding impossibility.

## The Five Iron-Clad Findings

These survived maximal skepticism from CRITIC, STATISTICIAN, and FACT-CHECKER. All have CRITIC confidence >= 0.87:

1. **The first 150 minutes rule** (Confidence: 0.97). Moving from sedentary to 150 min/wk MPA (8.75 MET-h/wk) is the single highest-value health behavior change. This captures the steepest benefit curve for 26 of 32 outcomes. Supported by A-Obs across 5+ outcomes and A-RCT for hypertension, depression, and falls.

2. **Combined aerobic + resistance > either alone** (Confidence: 0.92). HR 0.71 for ACM with combined vs. 0.77-0.78 with either alone (Stamatakis 2018, n=80K). Consistent across mortality, cancer mortality, metabolic, and mental health outcomes.

3. **VO2max/CRF is the strongest modifiable mortality predictor** (Confidence: 0.93). Each +1 MET = -13% ACM, -15% CVD mortality (Kodama 2009, n=103K). Elite fitness vs. low: HR 0.20 (Mandsager 2018, n=122K). Surpasses smoking, hypertension, and diabetes in predictive power.

4. **Exercise is first-line treatment for chronic pain** (Confidence: 0.92). Low back pain: 249 Cochrane RCTs, effect sizes rival NSAIDs without side effects. Knee OA: 54 Cochrane RCTs, equivalent to NSAIDs, safe for cartilage.

5. **Exercise is a moderate antidepressant, comparable to SSRIs** (Confidence: 0.87). Bias-adjusted SMD -0.50 (Schuch 2016). Network MA of 218 RCTs (Noetel 2024): all modalities effective. Lower relapse than pharmacotherapy (Blumenthal 2007).

## Three Dose Tiers (Universal)

All values in MET-h/wk with MPA-equivalent min/wk in parentheses.

| Tier | MET-h/wk | MPA-eq min/wk | What It Means | Coverage |
|------|----------|---------------|---------------|----------|
| **Universal Minimum** | 8.75 | 150 | 30 min brisk walking, 5x/wk | 26 of 32 outcomes show significant benefit. Captures 50-60% of maximum mortality benefit. |
| **Practical Optimal** | 15-20 | 250-350 | 150-200 min moderate + 60-90 min vigorous/wk | 80-95% of achievable benefit for most outcomes. |
| **Maximum Useful** | 25-40 | 430-690 | High volumes, mixed intensity | Only ACM and heart failure show continued (small) improvement beyond practical optimal. Diminishing returns for nearly all other outcomes. |

## What Exercise Does NOT Do (Null/Negative Findings)

Including these increases the credibility of the positive findings.

| Claim | Reality | Evidence |
|-------|---------|----------|
| Lowers LDL cholesterol | Minimal effect (-1 to -5 mg/dL, clinically insignificant). Affects HDL (+5-10%) and TG (-15-20%) instead. | A-RCT for non-response (Kelley 2004) |
| Improves cognition in established dementia | DAPA trial (n=494, BMJ): NO improvement. Exercise group slightly WORSE. | B-RCT null (Lamb 2018) |
| Causes meaningful weight loss alone | 1-3 kg at 6-12 months (Thorogood 2011). Diet alone: -4.6 kg. | A-RCT (Thomas 2012 Cochrane) |
| Stretching prevents injuries | RR 0.96 — no effect (Lauersen 2014, n=26,610). Strength training prevents injuries (RR 0.32, 68% reduction). | A-RCT |
